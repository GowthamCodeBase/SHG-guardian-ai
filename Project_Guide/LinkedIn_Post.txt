🚀 I just completed the Capstone Project for Kaggle's "5-Day AI Agents Intensive Course" with Google, and I'm excited to share the results!

For the past week, I've been deep in the world of Agentic AI—moving beyond single LLM prompts to design, build, and audit systems powered by autonomous cooperating agents. 

To put these skills to the test, I built 🛡️ SHG Guardian AI — a multi-agent risk auditing and forecasting platform for microfinance institutions and Self-Help Groups (SHGs).

💡 The Problem:
Many community-led microfinance groups rely on manual ledger entry, leading to delayed default warnings, duplicate transactions, and human error that puts group savings at risk.

🛠️ The Solution:
I built an agentic application using a Planner -> Worker -> Evaluator pattern. Instead of one monolithic script, 6 specialized agents coordinate using a custom protocol to scan transactions and assess credit risk.

Key Highlights of what I built:
🤖 6 Asynchronous Cooperating Agents (Planner, Anomaly Detector, Risk Assessment, Recovery Analysis, Approval, and Evaluator).
💾 SQLite Integration (Dual-layer short-term and persistent long-term memory for auditing).
⚙️ Custom Tools (Calculator tool, database execution tool, and predictive risk scoring heuristic).
⚖️ Human-in-the-Loop Safeguards (Flagged high-risk accounts are sent to a verification queue for Auditor approval).
📊 Gradio & Plotly UI (A clean interactive analytics dashboard running live).
